Shutting down

After more than two years, I'm making the decision to shut this project down.

I want to be honest about why, because I think people deserve more than a vague "lack of time" reason.

It ate my life

I started this as a side project when we were organizing syncs for 10 people. It stopped being one a long time ago. For two years it has been the thing I do instead of having a private life, instead of resting, instead of seeing people, instead of doing literally anything else with my evenings and weekends.

Every time I thought I could step back, another fire flared up: an apworld version that had to ship right now, an event that outgrew the tooling I'd built for the last one and forced me to half-rush something new before it started.

I told myself for a long time that I'd reach a stable plateau and the maintenance would calm down. It never did.

The community

I'll be diplomatic about this and just say: the Archipelago community is not a place I want to spend my free time anymore. Whether it's an age gap, a culture gap, or just the particular slice of people that gravitate to multiworlds, the entitlement and the bickering and the casual nastiness toward anyone giving away their time for free wore me down faster than any code ever did. Hosting a service for strangers is a very efficient way to discover how people behave when something is free.

There are some wonderful people in the community. Unfortunately there are also a lot who are not, and the ratio was not in my mental health's favor.

The upstream situation

There are several layers to this problem.

Core

The core team is clearly understaffed; PRs sit rotting for months (sometimes years) unless you happen to be in the right circle. I made a decision early on never to contribute upstream to core directly; instead I'd hand patches to people who were already in the trenches and let them carry them in. I am very thankful for these people (they know who they are) but this is not a happy situation.

Meanwhile the review bar for things that do get merged is, charitably, not high. I have personally reported code along the lines of json = requests.get("some_random_gist"); eval(json["foo"]), another similar to this in FFMQ and an ACE in LADX.

That is the actual standard being applied to code that runs on every player's machine. And that's the code that gets "reviewed". Now imagine the 500+ apworlds out there that don't even get looked at by people...

Security wise archipelago is a time bomb and I'm happy I won't be sitting next to it when it finally goes off.

Apworld devs

Archipelago has 300+ apworld developers at this point. Some are thoughtful, responsive, a pleasure to work with. Some are fine. And there are some who, in any other community I've been part of, would have been shown the door a long time ago for how they behave toward users, toward other devs, and toward anyone whose existence inconveniences them. They haven't been. They're still here, still treated as peers. Running infrastructure that has to interact with all of their work; and by extension, with them, even in a limited fashion; is something I am not willing to do anymore.

The last straw

For the most recent event I organized I spent 25 hours over two weeks doing reviews. There are 140+ PRs sitting open in the index repo right now, most blocked because the apworld can't generate reliably at the scale we run. Fine. That's the job I volunteered for.

What isn't the job: 90% of the DMs I got during that event were people asking for an apworld whose refusal was already on the repo, with a documented reason.

Linked from the lobby. Linked from the announcement. Under a bold "search before asking". Every single day I'd have the same conversation with a different person who hadn't read a single thing put in front of them.

Also not the job: arguing with apworld devs who think my standards are too strict. The standards aren't a vibe. At a thousand players, a 0.1% failure rate on every YAML means we're never generating; no one's playing. Math isn't in our favor.

I'm done.

What happens next

  • There will be no more apworld updates. I will archive the index repo.
  • I will disable new room creation on 2026-05-02 and link to this post instead.
  • After that, I will shut down this service on 2026-05-16. Get your YAMLs out before.

To everyone who used the lobby, who reported bugs without being awful about it, who helped answer people's questions, who said thanks: you were the good part. Thank you. I'm sorry it's ending this way, but it has to end.

Eije
